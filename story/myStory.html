Conservation Areas I've visited over the years

On this map, the markers are representing the conservation areas around my area and in fact I have visited over the past several years; also visiting them tens of times in that time frame.

Lynde Shores Conservation Area

A: Lynde Shores Conservation Area is my favourite conservation area to go to. The reason so is because of the diversity of birds that you can see throughout the area; along with that, you can feed birds using the bird feed stations that are around the area. In addition, with mix of wetlands and forest there is an abundant of wildlife such as, but not limited to, deer, racoons, ducks, swans, blue herons (uncommon), and many more.

Heber Down Conservation Area

B: Coming in at number 2: Heber Down Conservation Area. This conservation area has many trails with unique terrain to choose from and hike on. These trails have a certain level of difficulty to them from low-slope and cleared pathways to steep-slopes and undefined pathways. This is one of the main reasons I love going to this area as hiking difficulties are varied for trail to trail which brings a good challenge. Aside from the previous, the area has no shortage of wildlife and because of its proximity, more territorial animals are present which provides great spotting and awe.

Thicksons Woods

C: This conservation area is nothing short of unique as trails and areas within are access on a quite steep slope.
